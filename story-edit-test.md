# The Lantern in the Rain

On the last evening of autumn, Mara found an old brass lantern hanging outside a closed bakery.

The wind pushed cold rain across the street, and every window in the town had already gone dark. Mara almost walked past it, but the lantern gave a small, steady glow that did not flicker in the storm.

She lifted it from the hook and noticed a line engraved near the handle: "Carry this where someone is waiting."

Mara did not know who might be waiting, but the empty road felt less lonely with the lantern in her hand. She followed the narrow lane toward the hill outside town, where a single cottage stood between wet pine trees.

At the cottage door, an old man opened the door before she could knock. He looked at the lantern, smiled, and said, "I was hoping the light would remember the way."

He invited her in for tea. The room was warm, and the windows glowed gold against the rain. Before Mara left, the old man placed a fresh loaf of bread in her bag and told her to hang the lantern somewhere it might be needed again.

The next morning, Mara returned to the bakery. The hook was still there, shining in the pale sunlight. She hung the lantern carefully in its place and walked home with warm bread, wondering who the light would guide next.
